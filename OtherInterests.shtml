An Uncanny Floridian Obsession: Hockey


It began on the first game of the 1995-1996 professional hockey season. Actually it started less than hour before. In the Florida Panther locker room, while the players were preparing for the game, a rat scurried across room. While the more faint of heart screamed and jumped out of the way, Panther leader Scott Mellanby used a slap shot to send the rat into its next life. Using the same stick later that night in the game vs. the Calgary Flames, Mellanby recorded two goals including the game winner. John "Beezer" Vanbiesbrouck later called the occurrence a 'rat trick'. 1

As news of the rat trick made its way to the press, and subsequently to the fans, a new tradition was born. Two games later, as the Panthers defeated the Ottawa Senators 6 - 2, two rubber rats made their way onto the ice after a goal was scored. In the next game, 16 rats came raining from the stands. In the game after that: 50. By the time the playoffs came around, the rat count averaged over 2,000 each game. The Panthers went on to be one of the league's best hockey teams that year, claiming the Eastern Conference Championship. In the playoffs they were underdogs when they hammered the Boston Bruins in 5 games (I happened to be at game 5, and witnessed Bill Lindsay's timeless sliding-on-the-ice-breakaway-game-winning-late-third-period goal). Similarly, no one expected them to beat Eric Lindros and the power house Philadelphia Flyers in round 2. They did it in 6 games.

Finally, they faced Mario Lemieux, Jaromir (MarioJr rearranged) Jagr and the rest of the Pittsburg Penguins in an epic seven game series. They won again. The Panthers would have been Stanley Cup Champions that year, if not for a team that played in Denver, whose name will not be mentioned anywhere on this site (I'm not bitter).

Nostalgia sweeps over me just writing about that magnificent year. It is no coincidence that 1996 was also, by the Chinese calendar, "the year of the rat". That was when I became a Florida Panther fan. That was when I became a hockey fan. During the course of the year, in fact for Christmas, I got my first pair of skates and then my first hockey stick shortly after that.
